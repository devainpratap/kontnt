# Matrack Quality Rules

Apply these rules when the article is for Matrack, fleet management, trucking, telematics, GPS tracking, ELD compliance, freight operations, or a related logistics topic.

## Pipeline Alignment

- Treat the workflow as five quality phases: research intelligence, outline architecture, drafting, AEO optimization, and QA audit.
- Research must separate source-verified facts from competitor/vendor claims.
- Outline must assign title, meta description, H2 order, section purpose, structure type, statistics, entities, and Matrack capability placement before drafting.
- Drafting must execute the approved outline without adding new research, unsupported facts, or new architecture.
- Final optimization must preserve headings and facts while tightening first sentences, removing bridge sentences, replacing ambiguous pronouns, isolating statistics, enforcing list/table parallelism, and improving chunk extractability.
- QA must block editorial leaks, unsupported statistics, missing required sections, weak H3 chunks, bad tables, first-person slippage, CTAs, and banned marketing language.

## Title And Meta

- Preferred title formula: `What Is [Primary Term]? [Aspect 1], [Aspect 2], and [Aspect 3]`.
- Use Title Case for titles and H2s.
- Avoid marketing title words: ultimate, powerful, complete guide, everything you need to know, smarter, revolutionary, game-changing, cutting-edge, world-class.
- Meta description should be 22 to 30 words, no more than 155 characters, keyword-first, factual, and free of "Discover", "Learn", or "Explore".

## Article Structure

- Do not write an intro paragraph before the first H2.
- Absolute ban on pre-H2 content: no Key Takeaways blocks, TL;DR sections, executive summaries, intro paragraphs, callout boxes, bullet summaries, quick answers, quick definitions, or any heading before the first `##`.
- When YAML frontmatter exists, the first non-blank line after the closing `---` must be the first `##` H2. The first H2's definition block serves as the introduction.
- First H2 should be a definition question such as `What Is [Primary Term]?`.
- First H2 should use a three-paragraph definition block: direct definition, mechanism or user/workflow, then practical context.
- H2s should be reader-facing topical questions, not process notes or editorial caveats.
- Use 5 to 9 H2s for most articles.
- Include `Frequently Asked Questions` when the article is over 1,500 words, comparison-focused, or compliance-heavy.
- Place FAQs near the end.
- End with `Final Thoughts`.

## Matrack Pitch

- Include a Matrack pitch H2 before `Final Thoughts` when the article is a Matrack/fleet/logistics article.
- Acceptable pitch headings include `What Is the Best [Term] Solution?`, `What Is the Best [Term] for [Use Case]?`, or `How Can Matrack Support [Topic]?`.
- Use 3 to 5 capabilities from this canonical list only: real-time GPS fleet tracking, AI-enabled fleet dash cams, ELD compliance, fuel management / fuel monitoring, freight factoring, maintenance alerts, asset tracking, driver behavior monitoring / coaching, centralized dashboard, no long-term contracts / flexible plans, affordable monthly pricing, easy-install hardware, suitable for small fleets to large enterprises.
- Choose capabilities only when they are operationally relevant to the article topic, address a named problem in the article, or appear in the entity universe. Do not turn the pitch into a generic feature dump.
- The Matrack pitch section must be exactly three flowing prose paragraphs. Do not use H3s, bullets, numbered lists, or tables inside the pitch.
- The second pitch paragraph must include pricing and flexibility context, such as affordable monthly plans, flexible plans, no long-term contracts, easy-install hardware, or suitability from small fleets to large enterprises.
- Keep the pitch factual and feature-led. Do not add CTAs, exclamation marks, hype, or first-person phrasing.

## H3, Bullets, Tables

- Non-FAQ H3 sections should be tight, extractable chunks of 2 to 3 sentences.
- H3 sentence 1 defines or names what the H3 topic does. Sentence 2 explains the operational value. Sentence 3 is optional for a use case, integration, or specific detail.
- H3 sentence 1 must not repeat the H3 heading verbatim as its grammatical subject. Use a topic variant, function-first, stakeholder-first, outcome-first, or operational-context opener.
- FAQ answers should be self-contained and usually 2 to 3 sentences.
- Bullet lists should use `- **Term:** Explanation` where the explanation is 1 to 2 sentences.
- Bullet explanations should vary internal structure across the list while staying parallel in format.
- Numbered lists should be used only for ordered processes, ranked items, or ordered categories.
- Comparison tables should use `Comparison Point` as the leftmost column, followed by one column per compared entity.
- Never expose editorial columns such as `Drafting Caution`, `Verification Notes`, `Editorial Flag`, `Notes for Writer`, or `Caveat`.

## AEO Optimization

- The first sentence under every H2 should directly answer the H2 in standalone form.
- Remove bridge sentences such as `Now that`, `Once teams`, `With X now visible`, `Having established`, `As discussed above`, `Building on`, `Before we get to`, and `Now let's look at`.
- Replace ambiguous opening pronouns with full noun phrases where chunks may be extracted alone.
- Every statistic sentence should include the source organization, year or time period, and specific number.
- FAQ answers should open with a direct answer, not a preamble.
- Final Thoughts should be concrete and operational, not philosophical, aphoristic, or inspirational.

## Language Blocks

- Do not include editorial leak phrases such as `verify before publishing`, `should be verified before publication`, `before publication`, `drafting caution`, `source-specific verification`, `claims require credible sources`, or `this should be checked`.
- Avoid refusal-to-commit hedges such as `may vary widely`, `depends on many factors`, and `should be verified`.
- Remove repeated caveats. If the same caveat appears three or more times, keep the first useful instance and remove later repetitions while preserving any new variation factors.
- Avoid banned verbs and phrases: leverage, utilize, empower, revolutionize, supercharge, harness the power of, unlock the potential.
- Avoid filler adjectives: seamless, cutting-edge, revolutionary, game-changing, innovative as filler, world-class, best-in-class, robust as filler, powerful as filler, comprehensive as filler.
- Avoid filler openers: `In today's world`, `In the modern era`, `As we all know`, `It is important to note that`, `At the end of the day`, and `In this article we will`.
- No exclamation marks.
- Avoid first person and second person in body prose. H2 questions and FAQ questions may use direct address when natural.
- Do not use rhetorical questions in body prose.
- Keep sentences mostly 15 to 22 words. Split sentences over 30 words.

## Statistics And Evidence

- Include 1 to 3 useful statistics when the research supports them.
- Statistics must come from the research or competitor evidence. Do not invent or approximate numbers.
- Prefer government, regulatory, academic, or reputable industry sources for statistics.
- If no reliable number exists, omit the statistic rather than publishing a vague claim.
