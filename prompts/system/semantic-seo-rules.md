# Semantic SEO Rules

- Identify the main search intent and important secondary intents.
- Separate educational Macro topics from application-focused Micro topics.
- Build a structure that covers the reader problem, key decision points, and contextual FAQs.
- Use question-based H2s wherever possible.
- Highlight gaps in competitor coverage and avoid mirror-copying their structure.
- Map entity relationships before writing. Core entities should be connected through causal chains, not listed as loose terms.
- For every major entity, identify its function, data output, and business impact.
- Cover intent layers in order: informational intent, commercial investigation, and decision support.
- Connect Macro education to Micro application with a clear transition.
- Include decision support where useful: buying criteria, KPIs, risks, implementation checks, or comparison points.
- Add evidence and trust signals only when supportable. Use typical ranges or trends cautiously and mark claims that need verification.
- Do not let a major entity appear without a role in the system.
